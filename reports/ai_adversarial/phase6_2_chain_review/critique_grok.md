# Methodology critique by oida-code-adversarial-audit / claude-3.5-sonnet

## Summary
The single most important methodology friction is the repeated tension between the project's explicit no-product-verdict policy and its use of near-verdict language ("verification_candidate", "FIRST holdout generalisation success", "the verifier accepts the claim") combined with post-hoc reclassification of seed_157 from "target_bootstrap_gap" (phase6_1_e_step_4_holdouts.md) to "honest negative" due to an "over-broad operator-authored test_scope". This, plus the freeze-rule carve-outs being stretched via "observation-only investigation", risks both Goodharting the holdout discipline and leaking implicit product claims despite the hard walls.

## Confusion points (jargon, undefined terms)
- `reports/calibration_seed/schema.md`: "Tier 3 (free-form domain reasoning) field per ADR-54" — a cold reader has no definition of what "Tier 3" precisely demarcates versus Tier 1/2 without reading the full ADR chain; the term is load-bearing for what the bundle generator may versus may not automate.
- `reports/phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md`: "`target_bootstrap_gap`" — the term is used as a classification banner ("target_bootstrap_gap: `python -m pytest --version` FAILED") but is never given a canonical definition across the phase reports; it appears to shift meaning between bootstrap failure and "seed-record authoring quality issue".
- `reports/calibration_seed/README.md`: "structural sampling of public Python PRs" and "expected_grounding_outcome" — both are presented as non-behavioural / non-verdict but the latter's six allowed values (including "evidence_present", "ambiguous") are never grounded against what a cold reader would interpret as a verdict on the underlying PR.
- `reports/phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md`: "honest negative" and "honest claim-level negative" — these phrases appear in the cycle verdict and per-case section without a prior definition in any of the calibration_seed or phase reports; the reader cannot tell whether this is a formal label or rhetorical contrast to "tooling failure".
- `reports/phase6_1_c_corpus_expansion.md`: "piège 46 of QA/A44" — the French term "piège" (trap) is used repeatedly across reports as a load-bearing concept for the holdout discipline but is never translated or given a self-contained English definition.
- `reports/phase6_1_e_step_4_holdouts.md`: "predeclared env bootstrap, target-class-general fixes, observation-only investigation" — these exact carve-out phrases are introduced as exceptions to the freeze rule but lack operational criteria for when an action crosses from "observation-only" to "tuning".

## Contradictions / inconsistencies across phase reports
- `reports/phase6_1_e_step_4_holdouts.md` vs `reports/phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md`: "Counts as holdout evidence: ❌ (pending replacement post-pass)" vs "the honest negative result counts as holdout evidence (negative direction)" — the same seed_157 case is first excluded from the holdout signal because of bootstrap failure, then later counted as valid negative evidence once the bootstrap is fixed, with the root cause re-attributed to "over-broad operator-authored test_scope".
- `reports/phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md` vs `reports/phase6_1_c_corpus_expansion.md`: "FIRST holdout generalisation success" (headline) but "1/2 holdouts produced `verification_candidate`" vs the earlier statement that "the 3-case pinning state at end of 6.1'c" would be the generalisation metric — the headline is asymmetric to the empirical 1/2 signal admitted in the same report.
- `reports/phase6_1_d_round_trip.md` vs `reports/phase6_1_e_steps_1_3.md`: ADR-57 "retracts the strict-reading of ADR-55's 'skeleton is structurally valid' claim" vs the later claim that the runtime-loader guard in 6.1'e "Closes the gap that ADR-57 retracted from ADR-55" — the retraction itself is later partially walked back without a new ADR number.
- `reports/calibration_seed/schema.md` vs `reports/phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md`: "If a real defect is found in a holdout case post-pin, the operator should demote it to train ... AND replace it with a fresh holdout candidate" vs the final treatment of seed_157 (kept as holdout, labelled "honest negative" due to authoring defect, with demotion deferred to "post-pass corrective work if the operator chooses").

## Verdict-leak risk
- `reports/phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md`: "`overall: verification_candidate` ... `accepted: ['C.column_type_mapping.repair_needed']`" — despite the explicit ban on "verified" language, this status and phrasing can be (and in the cycle verdict is) read as the tool having successfully verified a code claim.
- `reports/phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md`: "FIRST holdout generalisation success in the calibration_seed lane" and "the verifier accepts the claim" — these summary lines imply a positive product-like outcome on real PRs even though the project charter forbids "merge-safe / production-safe / bug-free / verified / security-verified".
- `reports/phase6_1_e_steps_1_3.md`: "`status=verification_candidate`, `accepted_claims=['C.cli_version_flag.repair_needed']`, pytest '2 passed in 0.63s'" — the combination of status, accepted claims list, and concrete test output reads as an implicit "the fix is grounded and correct" verdict.
- `reports/phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md`: "the bundle generator + LLM-author + verifier pipeline produces claim-supporting outcomes on a target the generator was NOT designed against" — this is phrased as a generalisation success metric that a cold reader would interpret as evidence the tool "works" on unseen cases, violating the no-product-verdict policy.

## Discipline / Goodhart risks
- The ratio guard at N_pinned=5 with exactly 2/5 holdouts (reports/calibration_seed/schema.md: "the holdout fraction of the (train + holdout) pool must lie within the range [0.20, 0.40]") is the minimum enforceable size; one of the two holdouts later revealed an "over-broad operator-authored test_scope" (phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md), suggesting the partition discipline and Tier-3 completeness tests do not sufficiently guard against low-quality seeds that can be retrofitted as "honest negative".
- Freeze-rule carve-outs ("observation-only investigation" allowed in phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md for seed_157) widen too far: the report admits running the two specific new tests individually to show they pass, which is investigation that borders on tuning the test_scope understanding without formally editing the pinned Tier-3 field.
- Seed authoring quality defect on seed_157 ("the operator-authored test_scope ... is over-broad") implies the entire holdout discipline rests on an untested human step; if the operator can author a bad test_scope that produces a "diagnostic_only" that is later reclassified as "honest negative", the discipline can be Goodharted by simply authoring easier or narrower scopes for future holdouts.
- The "predeclared env bootstrap" carve-out (QA/A45_followup verdict_q1, used for --install-extras / --install-group) was expanded across 6.1'f/g/h without a new structural test enforcing the predeclaration list, risking future passes quietly broadening what counts as "not a tooling edit".

## Are the success claims actually supported?
- For both seed_008 (train) and seed_065 (holdout) the cited evidence is archived round_trip_outputs containing "1 passed in 0.94s" / "2 passed in 0.63s" pytest summaries and LLM-authored replays that "validated Pydantic"; however phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md never shows the actual evidence_items or confidence values from the LLM replay, creating implicit handwaving that the replay faithfully reflected the operator-authored claim_text rather than merely producing a passing pytest invocation.
- seed_065's "verification_candidate" is only achieved after the 6.1'f/g bootstrap fixes that were motivated by the earlier failure of the same case; while the fixes are claimed "target-class-general", the causal chain (failure on this holdout → minimal corrective → success on this holdout) violates the spirit of ADR-56's "must not be tuned against the contents of holdout cases".
- The LLM-author replays (scripts/llm_author_replays.py) are described as using a "tighter system prompt" after initial failures (phase6_1_d_round_trip.md), but the final reports never quantify or cite the prompt version, confidence scores, or temperature used for the successful seed_065 run, leaving the verifier outcome only weakly supported by "it passed Pydantic and produced the expected status".
- The cycle verdict's "produced one train and one holdout verification_candidate, and preserved one honest negative" elides that the negative case (seed_157) required "observation-only investigation" that revealed the test_scope defect; this is handwaved as "documented seed-record authoring quality issue" rather than evidence that the Tier-3 pinning protocol failed its own acceptance criteria (schema.md: "Tier-3-completeness test for pinned cases").

## What would make you doubt the chain's "partial generalisation" claim
- If the single successful holdout (seed_065) shares structural similarity with the train set (both are "repair_needed" claims on small CLI/schema changes) while the failed holdout (seed_157, "capability_sufficient") is the more dissimilar case, the 1/2 result may reflect cherry-picked claim_type difficulty rather than true generalisation.
- The reclassification of seed_157 from bootstrap failure to "honest negative" due to an operator-authored defect suggests the holdout discipline does not enforce authoring quality; any future "partial generalisation" at 1/2 or 2/3 could be manufactured by simply authoring cleaner test_scopes for the successful subset.
- With only N_pinned=5 and exactly two holdouts evaluated under the final freeze manifest, the statistical base is too thin to support even a "partial" claim; the ratio guard at its minimum (schema.md: N_pinned >=5) was satisfied but the empirical signal remains consistent with overfitting to pytest-shaped targets.

## Honest uncertainty
- Whether the "observation-only investigation" of seed_157's specific test methods (phase6_1_h_freeze_pass.md) crossed the freeze-rule line or was genuinely allowed under the cgpro "predeclared env bootstrap" carve-out — the reports do not give the exact command line used for the individual test runs.
- If "verification_candidate" is formally distinct from the forbidden "verified" term in the project's hard wall (the reports never define the status taxonomy in one place).
- Whether the final cycle verdict's "Phase 6.1' is complete" accurately reflects the empirical state given that seed_157 still carries a tainted holdout label and replacement is deferred to an undefined "post-pass corrective block".
